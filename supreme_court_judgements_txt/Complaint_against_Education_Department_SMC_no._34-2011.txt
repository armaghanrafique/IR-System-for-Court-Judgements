IN THE SUPREME APPELLATE COURT, GILGIT BALTISTAN SMC NO 34/2011 (Complaint against Education Department ) Advocate General Gilgit -Baltistan. 

Secretary, Education Department, Government of Gilgit -Baltistan. 

Director, Colleges, Government of Gilgit -Baltistan. 

Sher Ali, legal Advisor, Education Department. 

Date of Hearing: 20 -12-2011. 

ORDER Justice Muhammad Nawaz Abbasi, CJ: Mist, Sajida Yousaf, Saira Anwar and Naila Bibi residents of Gilgit -Baltistan have filed separate application before this court with th e grievance that despite their selection of merit on the basis of Test and interview conducted by the Education Department, Government of Gilgit -Baltistan for appointment as Teacher against the vacant posts in different schools of Gilgit -Baltistan they hav e not been appointed. 

The report called in these application has been submitted and Secretary Education appearing in person has explained that applicants have been selected for appointment but as per merits list they could not be appointed against the exis ting vacancies and their names have been kept on waiting list which is usually maintained for a period of one month and in the present case, the waiting list after 4th January, 2012 will be treated no more valid. 2. 

The Secretary; Education, Government of Gil git-Baltistan when point out that the waiting list is normally maintained for a period of 06 months, he conceded that in Punjab and other provinces of Pakistan the waiting list is maintained for a period of 6 months and agreed that the waiting list should also be maintained by the Education Department, Government of Gilgit -Baltistan for a period of six months. 

He stated that Department is facing shortage of about 2200 teachers in the schools and colleges throughout Gilgit -Baltistan but because of financial constraint and administrative reason, the department is handicapped to create the posts to meet the requirement.
