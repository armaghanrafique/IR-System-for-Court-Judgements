page in the supreme appellate court gilgit -baltistan, gilgit. rana muhammad shamim, chief judge. judge. cpla. no. khalid javed alias baba jan petitioner. haji mir baz khan others respondents. present: date of hearing: ... order. this leave to appeal is barred by time for sixty one days. the petitioner through civil misc. no. filed application under section of limitation act for condonation of delay. the learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner no. had gone to saudi arabia for performing hajj, therefore, cannot get certified copy of the impugned judgment well in time. he also submits that when the petitioner no. came back to gilgit at that time curfew was imposed in gilgit city and as such he could not file the petition within time. the learned counse for the petitioner further submits that the delay was neither intentional not deliberate, hence, the delay may very graciously be condoned. we have heard the learned counsel for the petitioner at length, perused the record of the case file and gone hrough the page courts of application under section of the limitation act. the perusal of the record transpires that the petitioner has enough time to get certified copy of the judgment before proceeding for hajj and after return from hajj but he failed to do so. the petition is admittedly barred by time for a period of months and day. the learned counsel for the petitioner could not offer plausible grounds reasons for filing the petition in hand after delay of considerable period of time. in view of the above and the law laid down by the apex court of pakistan in cases reported as scmr scmr scmr and scmr even one day unexplained delay was not condoned. the petition is converted into appeal and is di smissed. chief judge. judge.
